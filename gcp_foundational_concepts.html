Google Cloud Platform (GCP) is a suite of cloud computing services offered by Google. It provides scalable, secure, and highly available infrastructure for hosting applications, storing data, performing analytics, and more. GCP competes with other major cloud platforms like AWS and Azure and is known for its strengths in AI/ML, analytics, and network infrastructure.

GCP operates in multiple regions across the globe. Each region consists of multiple zones, which are isolated environments within the region. This design helps GCP provide fault tolerance, low latency, and high availability. Customers can deploy applications across zones for redundancy.

üîê Identity and Access Management (IAM)

IAM in GCP controls who (users or service accounts) can do what (roles and permissions) on which resources. It supports:

  • Principals: Users, groups, and service accounts
  • Roles: Predefined, custom, and basic (Viewer, Editor, Owner)
  • Policies: Binding of principals to roles

IAM provides fine-grained access control and is crucial for enforcing least-privilege security across your cloud resources.
